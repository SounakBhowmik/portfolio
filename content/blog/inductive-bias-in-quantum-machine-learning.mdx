## Why inductive bias matters

No model learns in a vacuum. In QML, the combination of feature maps, ansatz design, and observable choices defines the bias.

## A simple kernel view

For feature map $\phi(x)$ implemented by a quantum circuit, kernel methods use:

$$
K(x, x') = |\langle \phi(x) | \phi(x') \rangle|^2
$$

The geometry of this kernel decides what the model can generalize.

## Practical note

In experiments, hybrid models often win because they combine strong classical optimizers with quantum representations where useful.
