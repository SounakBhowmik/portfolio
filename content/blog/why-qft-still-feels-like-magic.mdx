## The phase perspective

The quantum Fourier transform (QFT) is often introduced as a matrix. In practice, I think about it as **rearranging phase information into measurable structure**.

$$
\text{QFT}\ket{x} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{N}}\sum_{k=0}^{N-1} e^{2\pi i xk/N}\ket{k}
$$

## Why it still surprises me

Even after repeated study, QFT feels magical because small phase differences become computationally useful through interference.

```python
import numpy as np

def qft_state(x, n):
    N = 2**n
    return np.array([np.exp(2j*np.pi*x*k/N)/np.sqrt(N) for k in range(N)])
```
